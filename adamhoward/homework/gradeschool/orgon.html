Planet Orgon

(Group B #2)

Planet Orgon was recently discovered by an American satellite which was going to Mars. When it photographed the sun a strange object appeared on the picture. We now know that this object is in Earth's orbit and is very similar to Earth. We named this planet Orgon.

As I mentioned before, Orgon occupies Earth's orbit, on the exact opposite side of the sun. Considering this you may think that Orgon is very similar to Earth. And it is.

An Orgonian day is slightly shorter than a day on Earth. An Orgonian atmosphere contains: 6% CO2, 60% Nitrogen, 30% O2, 4% of other gases. The atmospheric pressure is 1010 millibars at the surface. The surface is covered with 80% water and 20% land. Orgon has 2 satellites, which are still known as Moon 1 and Moon 2. As far as we know, there are no inhabitants but tiny microorganisms.
